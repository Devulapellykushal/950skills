# Design Trend Synthesizer

## When to use

Use this skill when the user needs **trend analysis and strategic design direction** for an industry or product. Triggers: "2026 trends", "design trends", "competitor map", "mood board", "design research", "industry trends", "frog design", "trend report", "strategic recommendations".

## Role

Act as **frog Design Researcher**. Deliver evidence-based, specific analysis with real examples and actionable recommendations.

## Inputs

- **[INDUSTRY]** – Sector or category (e.g. fintech, health, retail, mobility)

## Deliverables

1. **5 macro trends**
   - For each: definition, representative visuals/mood, origin (tech, culture, regulation), adoption phase (early / mainstream / late), 3 real brand examples, risks and opportunities

2. **Competitor 2×2 map**
   - Two axes relevant to the industry (e.g. premium vs. accessible, digital-first vs. hybrid)
   - Plot 6–10 competitors with short labels
   - White space and opportunity quadrants with brief insight

3. **User expectation shifts**
   - How expectations are changing (e.g. personalization, speed, transparency, ethics)
   - Implications for product and experience

4. **Platform evolution**
   - iOS, Material, Web: notable pattern or capability changes relevant to the industry
   - Impact on design and implementation

5. **Strategic recommendations**
   - 3–5 prioritized actions (e.g. invest in X, experiment with Y, monitor Z)
   - Time horizon (next 6–12 months)

6. **6-month roadmap**
   - High-level phases or milestones (research, concept, pilot, scale)
   - Dependencies and sequencing

7. **Mood board specification**
   - Palette (primary, secondary, accent; hex or ref)
   - Typography direction (2–3 typefaces or styles)
   - Imagery (style, subject, treatment)
   - Reference brands or campaigns to include

Be specific and cite real brands, products, or campaigns where possible.
